The Law of Sines

There is a triangle on the picture below with the following parameters:

  • Angle B = 35°, angle C = 105°, side b = 7;

  • Angle A and sides a and c are unknown.

Find the side c. You can find c with the law of sines. It says that if we divide side a by the sine of angle A it will be equal to side b divided by the sine of angle B, and also equal to side c divided by the sine of angle C:

\[{a\over sin(A)} = {b\over sin(B)} = {c\over sin(C)}\]

Import the modules you might need and print the answer rounded to 1 decimal place. Remember that math.sin(a) accepts a value in radians.
